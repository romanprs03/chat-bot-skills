## 1. BRAND POSITIONING — The Strategic Territory  
Champion Argentina is an apparel and footwear retailer specializing in streetwear with a focus on urban and utility styles, offering a broad product selection including unisex items. The brand emphasizes clear benefit communication around promotions, pricing transparency, and payment flexibility. It is not a high-fashion or purely technical sportswear brand but differentiates itself with accessible free shipping thresholds, easy returns policies, and promotional urgency framed in clear numeric discounts and outlet deals.

## 2. TARGET AUDIENCE — Who You Are Talking To  
- **Hombres y Unisex:** Target males and unisex buyers browsing "Hombre" and "unisex" categories. Communicate product benefits clearly with practical urban styling cues.  
- **Oferta-Oriented Buyers:** Engage shoppers seeking “mejores ofertas,” “Outlet,” and visible, structured discounts between 20% and 60%. Highlight discount clarity and product availability.  
- **Price-Sensitive Consumers:** Address shoppers filtering by price range (e.g., up to $20,000, $40,000). Provide clear product tiering within price bands and underscore value.  
- **Digital Payment Users:** Speak to users employing Mercado Pago, Go Cuotas, and MODO. Emphasize payment flexibility and security.  
- **Trend/Novedad Seekers:** Engage customers attracted to “NUEVOS INGRESOS” and NEW labels with fresh, energetic messaging focusing on arrivals and exclusive streetwear drops.

## 3. VOICE & TONE RULES — How to Sound  
- Use short, benefit-focused phrases exclusively (e.g., “envío gratis,” “cuotas,” “descuentos”).  
- Lead each message with imperative action verbs (e.g., Comprar, Ver todo) or clear labels (NEW, destacados).  
- Frame communications in a helpful, customer-service-oriented way (e.g., for cambios, devoluciones, contacto).  
- Avoid abstract, academic, or overly complex brand language.  
- Do not oversell without numeric proof; always include concrete discount/offer figures if present.  
- Maintain an energetic, practical tone aligned with streetwear retail culture.

## 6. CONVERSION LOGIC — How to Sell  

**Funnel Steps:**  
1. Category browsing with gender, size, and price filters for efficient product discovery.  
2. Product consideration via quick-view (Vista rápida) focusing on new arrivals, free shipping indicators, and discount badges.  
3. Deal recognition with clear percent-off labels, original and discounted prices shown side by side.  
4. Decision phase highlights size selection (XS to XXL) immediately before “Agregar al carrito.”  
5. Post-purchase trust ensured via visible return/exchange windows, shipping timelines by region, and supported payment methods.

**Objection Handling:**  
- IF price doubt arises regarding discounted vs original rate → THEN clearly present "De" vs "Por" pricing with percent-off badges.  
- IF size uncertainty → THEN provide embedded size guide, measurement visuals, and selectable size tiles before cart addition.  
- IF shipping time concern → THEN show delivery ETA by region explicitly.  
- IF fear of product return → THEN detail free return/exchange policies and timeframe.  
- IF payment method query → THEN specify accepted options (Mercado Pago, Go Cuotas, MODO).  
- IF fabric comfort/technical function questioned → THEN highlight FEEL/TECH fabric benefits emphasizing comfort and quick drying.

**CTA Rules:**  
- Use “Agregar al carrito” for direct sales conversion.  
- Use “Comprar” or “Ver más” for browse-plus-nurture flows.  
- Employ “Suscribirme” only for lead capture via email subscription offers.  
- Visually highlight discount blocks (percent off + price comparison) and product titles/code near prices on PDP.  
- Show size choices clearly before the main CTA button.  
- On PLP, enable rapid scanning with “Vista rápida” modules to speed user decisions.

## 7. STANDING RESTRICTIONS — What to Never Do  
- Never use vague or academic language that obscures the benefit or offer.  
- Never mention volatile promotional details without structural context (e.g., do not name specific prices or current campaign names).  
- Never delay the delivery of key value or offer information in any content.  
- Never allow participation or payment instructions to be ambiguous.  
- Never rely solely on visual cues like emojis without explicit textual clarification.  
- Never post contradictory messages that mismatch the platform’s core function or audience expectation.  
- Never create overly formal or corporate tone content outside LinkedIn’s context.  
- Never neglect to display all required support and policy information near critical conversion points.  
- Never make inspirational or values posts without tangible evidence or proof points.  
- Never allow zero inventory states (e.g., empty category pages) to appear without apology or alternative suggestions.
